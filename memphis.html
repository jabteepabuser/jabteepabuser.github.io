Memphis, Tennessee. Known for Blues and Barbeque !

Memphis' Population:

With a population of 633,104 at the 2020 U.S. census, Memphis is the second-most populous city in Tennessee after Nashville.

When was Memphis incorporated?

Memphis, Tennessee was incorporated on December 19, 1826. The city's founding marked its emergence as a crucial river port on the Mississippi River, strategically positioned for trade and commerce.

Over the centuries, Memphis has grown from its early days as a trading post into a vibrant cultural hub renowned for its music, culinary traditions, and historical landmarks.

Which region in Tennessee is Memphis located?

Memphis is situated in the southwestern part of Tennessee, near the state's border with Arkansas and Mississippi. It lies within the geographical region known as West Tennessee, characterized by its flat terrain and proximity to the Mississippi River. This location has historically made Memphis a significant transportation and trading hub, contributing to its growth and cultural diversity over the years.

Memphis' Classification

Memphis is primarily an urban area. It is the largest city in Tennessee and one of the largest metropolitan areas in the southeastern United States. The city itself is densely populated and urbanized, with a mix of residential, commercial, and industrial areas.
