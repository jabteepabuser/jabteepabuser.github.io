Nashville, Tennessee. The Home of Country Music and Nashville Hot Chicken.

Nashville's Population:

The current metro area population of Nashville in 2024 is 1,333,000, a 1.37% increase from 2023. The metro area population of Nashville in 2023 was 1,315,000, a 1.62% increase from 2022. The metro area population of Nashville in 2022 was 1,294,000, a 1.73% increase from 2021. The metro area population of Nashville in 2021 was 1,272,000, a 1.84% increase from 2020.

When was Nashville incorporated?

In 1806, Nashville was incorporated as a city and became the county seat of Davidson County, Tennessee. In 1843, the city was named as the permanent capital of the state of Tennessee. Knoxville, Kingston & Murfreesboro were prior locations of the state capital.

Which region in Tennessee is Nashville located?

Located in the Middle Tennessee Region consists of 33 counties east of the Tennessee River. The communities involved range from the smallest unincorporated areas to the capitol city of the state, Nashville.

Nashville's Classification

Nashville, Tennessee is classified as urban. It is the capital and largest city of Tennessee, known for its vibrant music scene (especially country music), cultural attractions, and economic significance within the region.
